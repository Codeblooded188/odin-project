Banku Recipe

Description

Banku is a traditional Ghanaian dish known for its hearty and satisfying nature. It's made from a mixture of fermented corn and cassava dough, resulting in a dense, smooth, and stretchy consistency. The dough is cooked by stirring it into boiling water until it thickens. Banku is often served with a variety of soups and stews, such as light soup, groundnut soup, or okra soup. The dish is typically enjoyed by tearing off a small piece of banku, shaping it into a scoop, and using it to pick up flavorful bites of the accompanying soup or stew, creating a delightful and culturally rich dining experience.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups of corn flour
  • 1/2 cup cassava flour
  • 5 cups water
  • 1 tablespoon salt

Steps

  1. Combine the corn and cassava flours in a large bowl.
  2. Add the water and salt to the corn and cassava flours.
  3. Stir to combine.
  4. Shape the dough into a ball.
